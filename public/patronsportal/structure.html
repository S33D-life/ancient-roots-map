Structure & Governance

A company that becomes infrastructure.

Papaya Seed Ltd is the bootstrap vessel. Over time, the gravity of value shifts from the company toward the commons — from shareholder extraction toward planetary stewardship. This page explains how, and why.

◯
The Framing

Not a venture-backed extraction loop. Regenerative infrastructure.

What we are building sits closer to a regenerative holding, treasury steward, and infrastructure builder than to a traditional startup forever extracting value from its users. The company is the vessel that gets the network into the world. The network is what the value migrates to.

The long-term aim is not to centralise value extraction, but to grow a planetary commons.

I
The Three Phases

Formation. Treasury. Transition.

The structure evolves in three movements — each laying the ground for the next. Investor exposure remains aligned with ecosystem health at every step.

Phase One Formation

Papaya Seed Ltd — the bootstrap vessel.

A dormant shell activated for EIS/SEIS-compatible investment. Receives Staff Patron funds, hires builders, develops the core infrastructure, owns early IP and contracts, and operates app infrastructure. Revenue arrives through subscriptions, donations, Staff patronage, and early partnerships.

  • Subscriptions — Monthly, Annual, Decade Ring
  • Staff patronage — founding tier donations
  • Early partnerships — schools, councils
  • Core infrastructure development
  • Early IP and contracts
  • App and platform operations
Phase Two Treasury Exposure

The company accumulates ecosystem positioning.

Papaya Seed builds a strategic reserve of S33D Hearts, governance positioning, treasury exposure, ecosystem equity, Staff holdings, and infrastructure ownership. Shareholder value becomes indirect exposure to ecosystem growth — rather than extracting value directly from users in perpetuity. This is a very different philosophy.

  • Strategic Hearts treasury reserve
  • Governance positioning
  • Ecosystem equity stakes
  • Staff holdings — lineage rights
  • Infrastructure ownership
  • Platform revenue streams
Phase Three Ecosystem Transition

The company becomes a regenerative infrastructure fund.

Most value migrates into S33DAO, Grove DAOs, Species DAOs, tree treasuries, pod economies, and Hearts circulation. Papaya Seed evolves into a fund that invests in ecosystem projects, supports pod formation, builds tooling, funds public goods, and maintains the core stack the network rests on.

  • Invests in ecosystem projects
  • Supports pod formation
  • Builds and maintains tooling
  • Funds public goods
  • Incubates grove economies
  • Supports governance transitions

The company benefits if the ecosystem flourishes. But the ecosystem is not permanently extractable by the company.

V
The Hearts — Framing Matters

What Hearts are. What Hearts are not.

Hearts are governance, coordination, stewardship, and ecological commons currency. They are not, at launch, framed as investment returns. Secondary value may emerge naturally — but the framing must hold.

What Hearts are

  • Governance coordination
  • Stewardship incentives
  • Participation infrastructure
  • Ecological commons currency
  • Treasury alignment
  • Ecosystem utility

What Hearts are not

  • Investment returns
  • Speculation tokens
  • Yield farming instruments
  • Tree NFTs as collectibles
  • Web3 financialisation
  • Extractive shareholder value
VI
The Deeper Layer

Rebuilding the commons through living memory infrastructure.

Tokens, NFTs, and chains are implementation layers. The deeper layer is older — ecological continuity, intergenerational archives, place-based stewardship, community coordination, regenerative treasury systems.

Principle 01 Ecological continuity The living world is the substrate. Every record anchors to a real tree, in a real place, that has been there longer than us.
Principle 02 Intergenerational archives Memory is built to survive companies, platforms, and decades. The archive deepens — it does not lapse.
Principle 03 Place-based stewardship Value emerges where people physically meet trees, learn, and tend. Local commons, not global feed.
Principle 04 Regenerative treasuries Capital pools are connected to living places. As the ecosystem grows, so does the treasury that funds its continued public infrastructure.

Rare. And increasingly attractive to regenerative capital, aligned DAOs, mission-driven investors, and long-horizon funds.
